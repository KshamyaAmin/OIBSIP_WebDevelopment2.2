1907 - 1931

A Revolutionary Spirit

Bhagat Singh stands as one of the most influential and inspiring revolutionaries in India's freedom struggle. Born on September 28, 1907, in Banga, Punjab, he grew up in a family deeply involved in the independence movement, which shaped his revolutionary ideals from a young age.

What makes Bhagat Singh truly remarkable is not just his courage, but his intellectual depth and progressive thinking. He was a voracious reader, studying works on socialism, communism, and revolutionary movements worldwide. His vision extended beyond mere political independence; he dreamed of a free India built on principles of equality, justice, and secularism.

At the tender age of 23, Bhagat Singh was hanged by the British colonial government on March 23, 1931, along with his comrades Rajguru and Sukhdev. His sacrifice ignited a fire in the hearts of millions of Indians and accelerated the momentum of the freedom movement. He faced death with extraordinary courage, singing patriotic songs as he walked to the gallows.
